Getting started

Get yourself run ready

If you’re new to running or haven’t done much exercise for a while, then it’s very important to start gradually. Start with the small things like walking to work or the shops, taking the stairs instead of the lift and just being a little more active.

If you’re starting an exercise program and you are either over 40, overweight or have a family history of heart disease. Most GPs will be more than happy to offer advice and will most likely be delighted that you are thinking about your health.

It can be hard to get into a habit of running. Often we struggle to see how we can fit it into our day. But most of us will be able to find a small window, and we should try and make it a priority of commit to using it. It’s so easy to things get in the way. Be firm!

It often helps new runners to try to start their runs with a brisk walk to ease you in. Speed up into a slow jog when you feel ready, and walk again for a while if you feel tired. After a short time doing this, you’ll find that you are able to run more than you need to walk. You’ll be well on your way to being a real runner in no time!
